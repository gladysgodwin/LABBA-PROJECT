Who is a Laboratory Scientist?

-----------------------------------

A medical laboratory scientist (MLS), also known as a medical technologist or clinical laboratory scientist, works to analyze a variety of biological specimens. They are responsible for performing scientific testing on samples and reporting results to physicians.
Medical laboratory scientists perform complex tests on patient samples using sophisticated equipment like microscopes. The data they find plays an important role in identifying and treating cancer, heart disease, diabetes, and other medical conditions. It is estimated 60 to 70 percent of all decisions regarding a patient's diagnosis, treatment, hospital admission, and discharge are based on the results of the tests medical laboratory scientists perform.

Who is a Laboratory Technician and Phlebotomist?

Laboratory technicians perform a variety of tasks in clinical and medical settings ranging from drawing blood to performing lab tests.
Laboratory technicians collect samples of body fluids and tissues and perform clinical tests analyzing these samples. These tests are usually of a simpler nature than those performed by laboratory technologists or scientists, who have more advanced training and often oversee and instruct technicians.They are responsible for maintaining and sterilizing equipment.
Some specialties exist within the laboratory technology field. Phlebotomists, for example, are primarily concerned with the collection of the blood samples used for laboratory tests, blood donations and transfusion procedures. Histotechnicians, another specialty of laboratory technicians, are responsible for preparing the microscopic slides of tissue specimens that pathologists use to make their diagnoses.
